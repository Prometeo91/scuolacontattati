What data we collect

We only collect data that you give us voluntarily or that your browser automatically sends during normal website browsing.

Data you provide

When you fill in the contact form we collect:

  • Name (required) — so we can reply to you by name
  • Email address (required) — to send you a reply
  • Message (required) — the content of your request
  • Phone (optional, if you include it) — only if you prefer to be contacted by voice

Automatically collected data

Like any website, we receive basic technical information from your browser: browser type, operating system, language, referring page, date and time of visit. If you have consented to analytics cookies, we also collect aggregated and anonymised data on how you browse (see the Cookies section).

We do not collect precise geolocation data, biometric data, health data, or data of any of the special categories referred to in Art. 9 GDPR.

Retention periods

  • Contact form messages: retained for up to 24 months from the last interaction, then deleted. If you become a student of the School, the data follows the educational relationship for the strictly necessary time.
  • Google Analytics data: retention set to 14 months, then automatically deleted by Google.
  • Technical logs (IP, security): retained for a maximum of 6 months.

Recipients and data transfers

Your data remains accessible only to those who need to process it for the purposes listed above. In particular:

  • The School's conductors (Anna Carla, Nicolaos), who read and reply to messages.
  • The contact form provider (the site's email delivery service), which receives the message to deliver it to us.
  • Google Ireland Ltd. for the Google Analytics 4 service (only if you have given consent). Google may transfer data to non-EU countries; the transfer is based on the Standard Contractual Clauses approved by the European Commission.

We do not share your data with third parties for commercial, advertising, or profiling purposes.

Cookies and analytics tools

This site uses two types of cookies:

Technical cookies (always active)

These are the cookies strictly necessary for the site to function. We save two preferences in your browser via localStorage:

  • sctheme — your theme choice (light/dark), so we don't ask you on every visit.
  • sc-cookie — your cookie banner choice (accepted / declined), so we don't show it again.

For these cookies your consent is not required, as they are essential to provide you with the requested service (Art. 122 Italian Privacy Code).

Analytics cookies (with consent)

Only if you accept via the banner do we activate Google Analytics 4 with these enhanced privacy settings:

  • Anonymised IP — Google receives a truncated IP, not traceable to you.
  • No advertising — we do not share data with Google's advertising services (Ad Storage denied).
  • Consent Mode v2 — until you make a choice, no analytics are triggered.
  • 14-month retention — the minimum configurable.

Google Analytics helps us understand, in aggregate form, which sections of the site are useful and which are confusing — so we can improve them. Nothing more.

Your rights

The GDPR grants you precise rights over your data. At any time you can ask us to:

  • Access the data we hold about you (Art. 15)
  • Rectify inaccurate or incomplete data (Art. 16)
  • Erase your data ("right to be forgotten", Art. 17)
  • Restrict processing in specific cases (Art. 18)
  • Receive your data in a structured and portable format (Art. 20)
  • Object to processing based on legitimate interest (Art. 21)
  • Withdraw consent at any time, without affecting the lawfulness of previous processing (Art. 7)

To exercise any of these rights, simply write to us at . We will respond within 30 days, personally, without forms or bureaucracy.

Complaint to the supervisory authority

If you believe that the processing of your data is in breach of the GDPR, you always have the right to lodge a complaint with the Garante per la protezione dei dati personali (Italian Data Protection Authority): www.garanteprivacy.it.
